Defining Full-Stack Development

A progressive approach to software development requiring one to have detailed knowledge of the entire Software / Solution Stack. Requiring proficiency in a variety of languages & frameworks.

The term Full-Stack refers to the stack of multiple technologies that configures, compiles, and ultimatley serves up your application. Having the experience and knowledge of multiple languages, frameworks, SDKs / APIs, and the latest coding standards & best practices.

This does NOT imply that a full-stack developer has mastered everything within the overall SDLC; it DOES imply however, that he or she has the skills needed to weave in / out of each layer of the Stack comfortably with minimal assistance.

Overview: Pillars of Focus

It's safe to say that no two applications are alike. So when approaching any new project, expect a specific set of requirements and dependencies. Keeping that in mind, I consider the following four arenas as critical pillars when forgingconcepts into creation!
Performance

Payload Speeds

Fast & efficent delivery of your cloud / web-based application is always a top priority - As well as maintaining a latencey-free user experience once delivery / loading has completed.

Progressive

Responsive Designs

Creating a fully responsive user-interface (UI) that will scale gracefully across any device / platform - With emphasis on leveraging the latest frameworks / libraries for scaffolding & overall styling.

Intuitive

Modular & Scalable

Putting thought into every element to ensure the best user-experience (UX) possible. All the while building a component driven experience that will scale seamlessly with your business needs.

Dynamic

Interactive UI/UX

Having a clean interface doesn't mean that it has to be static and boring - Adding in purposeful interactions and finding creative ways to display & show-off your data is one of my strengths.
